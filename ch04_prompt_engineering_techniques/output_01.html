# One-Day Meal Plan for Endurance Runner with Glycogen Loading

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete preparing for a 15km morning run followed by an 8km afternoon run with lactose intolerance and digestive comfort needs:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 2,800 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (105g), Fat (65g), Carbs (425g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Pre-Dawn Snack (5:30 AM - 2 hours before 15km run):** White rice (80g cooked) with honey (20g) and banana (120g).
  * Protein: 3g, Fat: 1g, Carbs: 65g

* **Breakfast (8:00 AM - Post 15km run):** Oatmeal (70g dry, made with water, 200g) topped with maple syrup (25g), sliced strawberries (100g), and coconut oil (10g). Orange juice (250g).
  * Protein: 6g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 85g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:30 AM):** Rice cakes (40g) with almond butter (25g) and dates (40g).
  * Protein: 8g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 50g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Grilled chicken breast (140g) with jasmine rice (150g cooked) and steamed carrots (120g). Light olive oil drizzle (10g).
  * Protein: 38g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 65g

* **Pre-Afternoon Run Snack (3:30 PM - 30 minutes before 8km run):** Sports drink (500g coconut water) with banana (100g).
  * Protein: 2g, Fat: 0g, Carbs: 35g

* **Post-Run Snack (5:00 PM):** Rice pasta (120g cooked) with olive oil (15g), garlic (5g), and steamed zucchini (150g). Salt to taste.
  * Protein: 5g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 50g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Baked white fish (150g) with sweet potato (200g) and steamed green beans (150g). Olive oil (8g).
  * Protein: 35g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 60g

* **Evening Snack (9:00 PM):** Rice crackers (35g) with dairy-free spread (20g) and apple (120g).
  * Protein: 3g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 30g

---

**Key Features:** This plan prioritizes easily digestible white rice, oatmeal, and simple carbohydrates for optimal glycogen loading. All dairy products are eliminated, and meals are timed strategically around both runs to maximize performance and recovery while maintaining digestive comfort.
